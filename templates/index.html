Water DaMS – Web Portal

Water DaMS is a comprehensive web-based water portal, developed to streamline the data-management of waterbodies and its conservation/restoration works as well as to provide real-time status monitoring of rejuvenation activities. This unique digital tool empowers stakeholders / government officials to make informed decisions in managing waterbodies across the district of Madurai.

HCLFoundation

DHAN Foundation

Development of Humane Action (DHAN) Foundation, is a national-level professional development institution, promoted with the mission of “Building People and Sustainable Institutions with Value Driven, Ethics, and Democratic process to enable the poor for Poverty Eradication, Water & nutrition secured; Inclusive and Ecologically Balanced Development”... DHAN Foundation through its water thematic Programme (DHAN Vayalagam Tank Foundation) has facilitated people institutions to restore 4000+ traditional water commons such as minor irrigation tank cascade systems, drinking water ponds, community wells, and Ahar-Pyne systems. In the past 25 years, DHAN Foundation has been working towards documenting, preserving, and revitalizing the indigenous wisdom on water management. It also imparts technological solutions such as remote sensing and GIS in water resources management at the grassroots. In Building Water Portal Madurai Chapter, DHAN Foundation is partnering with HCL Foundation by providing support in technical aspects such as digitization, and development of questionnaires, co-creating applications and portals with the relevant stakeholders, and testing and implementing at the grassroots.

Ponds & Ooranis

Ponds, locally known as Oorani, are small water bodies typically used for domestic purposes, livestock, and community needs in rural areas. They play a crucial role in recharging groundwater and providing water during dry seasons.

Temple Tanks

Temple tanks are sacred water reservoirs associated with temples, used for religious rituals and community events.

Irrigation Tanks

Irrigation tanks are larger water bodies designed for agricultural purposes, helping to store rainwater and provide irrigation through an interconnected network of channels.

F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What are the major types of surface water bodies?

    There are four types of surface water bodies: Irrigation tanks, Ponds, Ooranis, and Temple tanks.

  • What are system and non-system tanks?

    Tanks that receive water supply from some other supplemental source, like a river or reservoir, through a supply channel are called system tanks. Because of supplemental sources, water storage in system tanks generally lasts longer than in non-system tanks or rainfed tanks. Tanks that receive water supply through runoff, only from their catchments (both free and combined catchments) and the surplus from the above tanks, all of which depend only on rainfall-runoff, without any other supplemental source are known as non-system or rainfed tanks.

  • What is meant by Perennial and Seasonal water bodies?

    Perennial waterbodies are those that bear water throughout the year while seasonal waterbodies have water only during a particular period, i.e., the rainy season.

  • How does climate change affect water bodies?

    Climate change affects water bodies through rising sea levels, changes in precipitation patterns, increased evaporation rates, and more frequent and intense droughts and floods.

  • Why are water bodies important?

    Water bodies are crucial for ecosystems, biodiversity, agriculture, human survival, groundwater recharge, and climate regulation.

  • What are the major threats to water bodies?

    Encroachment and Solid waste disposal.

  • What is Inlet and outlet structure ?

    Inlets and outlets are features of waterbodies that allow water to flow into and out of the waterbody, and limit the rate at which water flows. Inlet structures are built to control the amount of water flowing into the water body at all times. The outlets or drainage pipes are installed at the lowest elevation of the bottom of the water body. It has 4 types namely Open Channel, simple conduit, Culvert, and Culvert with shutter.

  • What is silt trap?

    A silt trap is a technique that prevents silt and other unwanted materials from entering a water storage system or drainage system. Silt traps work by temporarily holding water so that silt and other materials can settle to the bottom of the trap and separate from the water. The filtered water can then continue on its way through the system.
